# NomNomGo Principles

## Product Principles

- People first.
- Real-world experiences over screen time.
- Planning before messaging.
- Groups before individuals.
- Suggestions before searching.
- Transparency before algorithms.
- AI assists. Humans decide.
- Simple beats clever.
- Fast beats perfect.

## Planning Principles

- Every plan should have a clear next action.
- Every participant should know how to contribute.
- Every recommendation should explain why it appeared.
- Every locked plan should be easy to share, save, and revisit.
- Planning history should reduce future planning effort.

## Social Principles

- Relationships are earned through repeated useful planning, not feed mechanics.
- Groups should feel lightweight at first and durable only when repeated use justifies it.
- Privacy and consent matter more than growth loops.
- Social features should make coordination easier, not create social pressure.

## Recommendation Principles

- Recommendations should start useful before they become personalized.
- Group fit is more important than individual taste optimization.
- Explicit feedback beats hidden inference.
- Sponsored placement must be labeled clearly.
- Users should be able to understand and correct preference assumptions.

## AI Principles

- AI should reduce planning labor, not remove human agency.
- AI suggestions must be explainable and reversible.
- AI actions that affect people, businesses, money, time, or reservations require clear confirmation.
- Agent integrations should use stable planning APIs rather than browser scraping or hidden workflows.

## Engineering Principles

- Keep NomNomGo independently deployable.
- Do not build permanent dependencies on Differance Labs auth.
- Do not expose server-side secrets to the browser.
- Prefer small, testable changes.
- Reliability is product work.
- Instrument the planning funnel before optimizing it.

## Business Principles

- Monetization should make planning better.
- Businesses can influence, but not secretly manipulate, recommendations.
- Revenue models must preserve user trust.
- Venue tools should work for small operators before enterprise features are added.
